Intro (Opening Hook)

Mushroom farming is one of the easiest and most profitable small-scale agricultural businesses.

It requires very little land, low investment, and gives quick returns.

In this video, you will learn how to grow oyster mushrooms step-by-step, from preparing the straw to harvesting the mushrooms.

Section 1: What is Oyster Mushroom Farming

Oyster mushrooms are one of the most commonly cultivated edible mushrooms.

They grow well in warm and humid environments.

Compared with other mushrooms, oyster mushrooms are easier to cultivate and ideal for beginners.

Section 2: Materials Needed

To start mushroom cultivation, you need:

Mushroom spawn (mushroom seeds)

Corn cob powder or dried agricultural straw (used as the growing medium)

Plastic growing bags

long size for water filtering bag

Plastic water storage drum

Lime powder

Bleaching powder

Clean water

A dark room with proper humidity

Section 3: Preparing the Growing Medium (Corn Cob)

The growing medium must be prepared properly before spawning to prevent contamination.

Steps:

Place the plastic water storage drum outside the farming area to keep the preparation process separate from the cultivation space.

Fill the drum with about three-quarters (3/4) of water.

In a separate container, take 5 liters of water and mix lime powder and bleaching powder thoroughly.

Pour this chemical mixture into the plastic water storage drum and mix it well with the water inside the drum.

Take three long bags and fill them with corn cob material.

Submerge the three corn cob bags completely inside the drum so they soak in the treated water.

Leave the bags soaked for 12–24 hours to allow proper sterilization.

After 12–24 hours, release the water from the plastic storage drum.

Remove the corn cob bags and drain and dry the corn cob until it becomes slightly moist, making it ready for the bagging and spawning process.

This step is important because contamination can ruin the entire crop.

Section 4: Bag Preparation and Spawning

Now the mushroom bags are prepared.

Steps:

Place a layer of straw inside the plastic bag.

Add a layer of mushroom spawn.

Repeat the layers until the bag is full.

Tie the bag tightly.

Make small holes in the bag for air and mushroom growth.

Section 5: Incubation Stage

After bag preparation:

Place the bags in a dark and humid room.

Avoid direct sunlight.

Maintain proper moisture and ventilation.

Within 10–15 days, white fungal growth called mycelium spreads through the straw.

Section 6: Mushroom Growth

Once mycelium spreads fully:

Small mushroom buds begin to appear from the holes.

Mushrooms grow rapidly within a few days.

Proper humidity helps increase yield.

Section 7: Harvesting

Mushrooms are ready when the caps are fully grown.

Harvest by gently twisting the mushroom cluster from the base.

Avoid pulling too hard to prevent damage.

Section 8: Selling and Profit

After harvesting:

Mushrooms can be sold in local vegetable markets.

Restaurants and hotels are also good buyers.

Fresh mushrooms have good demand and price.

Closing

Mushroom farming is a practical and profitable business opportunity.

With proper hygiene and environmental control, beginners can start small and gradually expand production.
